Fushimi Inari Shrine

Fushimi Inari Shrine is a very important Shinto shrine which is found in southern Kyoto1. It is famous for its thousands of vermilion Torii gates, which straddle a network of trails behind its main buildings2. The trails lead into the wooded forest of the sacred Mount Inari, which is a mountain that stands at 233 meters and belongs to the shrine grounds3.

I was fascinated by this particular shrine, found in Japan due to its history as well as the legend that surrounds it4. Inari is a deity, supposedly known for being the god of harvests and foxes which intrigues me because I am curious to know more about Japan's folklore5.
